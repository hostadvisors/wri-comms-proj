<CardGrid>
  <Card title="The Citizen Voice Defined" icon="information">

## When to Use the Citizen Voice

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Key Applications">
    <Card title="Primary Use Cases" icon="list">
      :::note[Best For]
      - **All-staff announcements** that affect the entire organization
      - **Policy updates** and procedural changes
      - **Community-building initiatives** (DEI, events)
      - **Organizational updates** during transitions
      - **Resource sharing** and guidance
      - **Crisis communications**
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Communication Goals">
    <Steps>
      1. **Build Trust** through consistent, reliable messaging
      2. **Foster Community** by emphasizing shared values
      3. **Ensure Clarity** with actionable information
      4. **Promote Inclusion** across all levels and regions
      5. **Drive Engagement** while maintaining professionalism
    </Steps>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

:::tip[When to Choose the Citizen Voice]
Use this voice when you need to build trust, foster community understanding, or deliver important organizational messages that affect the entire WRI team.
:::

## Core Characteristics of WRI's Citizen Voice

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Reliable & Steady">
    <Card title="Building Trust Through Consistency" icon="verify">
      <Steps>
        1. Use consistent messaging across channels
        2. Follow through on communication commitments
        3. Provide regular, scheduled updates
        4. Present information logically
      </Steps>

      :::tip[Example Language]
      - "We're committed to keeping you informed throughout this process"
      - "As promised, here's the update on our progress"
      - "You can count on receiving these updates every month"
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Inclusive & Welcoming">
    <Card title="Creating a Sense of Belonging" icon="people">
      <Steps>
        1. Use collective language ("we," "our," "together")
        2. Acknowledge global perspectives
        3. Avoid insider language
        4. Consider cultural contexts
      </Steps>

      :::tip[Example Language]
      - "Each of us plays a vital role in this initiative"
      - "We recognize that our teams across different regions may experience this differently"
      - "Whether you're new to WRI or have been here for years, this affects us all"
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Practical & Solution-Focused">
    <Card title="Delivering Clear Action Steps" icon="right-arrow">
      <Steps>
        1. Lead with key information
        2. Include specific next steps
        3. Provide clear resources
        4. Address common concerns
      </Steps>

      :::tip[Example Language]
      - "Here's what this means for your day-to-day work"
      - "To get started, simply follow these three steps"
      - "If you have questions, contact [specific person/team] by [date]"
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Honest & Transparent">
    <Card title="Building Trust Through Openness" icon="open">
      <Steps>
        1. Acknowledge complexity and uncertainty
        2. Share decision-making context
        3. Be upfront about challenges
        4. Focus on solutions and next steps
      </Steps>

      :::tip[Example Language]
      - "We're still working through some details, and we'll update you as we know more"
      - "This decision wasn't easy, and here's the thinking behind it"
      - "We've learned from this experience and here's how we're improving"
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Tone Guidelines by Communication Type

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Policy Updates">
    <Card title="Policy & Procedural Communications" icon="document">
      <Steps>
        1. Use clear, direct language about requirements
        2. Explain the reasoning behind changes
        3. Acknowledge potential impact on teams
        4. Provide implementation support resources
      </Steps>

      :::note[Framework Example]
      "We're updating [policy] to better serve our team and mission. Here's what's changing and why it matters to all of us..."
      :::

      :::tip[Best Practice]
      Balance authority with approachability to ensure compliance without alienation.
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Organizational Changes">
    <Card title="Change Management Communications" icon="refresh">
      <Steps>
        1. Lead with positive purpose and outcomes
        2. Address likely concerns proactively
        3. Emphasize continuity alongside change
        4. Provide clear timelines and next steps
      </Steps>

      :::note[Framework Example]
      "As we continue to grow and evolve, we're making changes that will help us serve our mission more effectively..."
      :::

      :::tip[Best Practice]
      Provide stability and reassurance during periods of transition.
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Recognition">
    <Card title="Celebrations & Recognition" icon="star">
      <Steps>
        1. Connect achievements to collective success
        2. Share specific, learnable examples
        3. Link to organizational values
        4. Enable team engagement and congratulations
      </Steps>

      :::note[Framework Example]
      "Let's celebrate [achievement] and recognize how this reflects the excellent work happening across our organization..."
      :::

      :::tip[Best Practice]
      Create inclusive celebrations that strengthen community bonds.
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Crisis Communications">
    <Card title="Difficult Communications" icon="warning">
      <Steps>
        1. Start with facts and current status
        2. Acknowledge human impact
        3. Outline concrete action steps
        4. Provide regular updates
        5. Offer support resources
      </Steps>

      :::note[Framework Example]
      "We want to keep you informed about [situation]. Here's what we know, what we're doing, and how we'll support each other through this..."
      :::

      :::tip[Best Practice]
      Provide calm leadership while acknowledging real concerns.
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Language and Style Guidelines

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Word Choice Guide" icon="pencil">
    :::tip[Use These Words]
    - "We" and "our" (collective identity)
    - "Together," "shared," "common" (unity)
    - "Practical," "straightforward," "clear" (accessibility)
    - "Support," "resources," "guidance" (helpfulness)
    - "Consistent," "reliable," "steady" (dependability)
    :::

    :::caution[Avoid These Words]
    - "Exciting," "amazing," "incredible" (overly promotional)
    - "Obviously," "clearly," "of course" (potentially condescending)
    - "Cutting-edge," "revolutionary," "game-changing" (too sales-oriented)
    - Complex jargon without explanation (exclusionary)
    :::
  </Card>

  <Card title="Writing Structure" icon="open-book">
    <Steps>
      1. **Use Active Voice**
         - ✅ "We are implementing..."
         - ❌ "Changes are being implemented..."
      
      2. **Keep It Clear**
         - Write concise sentences
         - Lead with key information
         - Use parallel structure in lists
    </Steps>
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Card title="Accessibility Best Practices" icon="accessibility">
  <Tabs>
    <TabItem label="Document Structure">
      - Use clear heading hierarchy
      - Organize with logical sections
      - Include descriptive headings
      - Maintain consistent formatting
    </TabItem>

    <TabItem label="Content Formatting">
      - Use bullet points for lists
      - Highlight key deadlines
      - Add clear subject lines
      - Include action items
    </TabItem>

    <TabItem label="Visual Elements">
      - Ensure sufficient contrast
      - Use bold for emphasis
      - Include alternative text
      - Create scannable layouts
    </TabItem>
  </Tabs>
</Card>

## Communication Channels & Best Practices

<Tabs>
  <TabItem label="Email Communications">
    <Card title="Email Best Practices" icon="mail">
      <Steps>
        1. Write clear, action-oriented subject lines
        2. Lead with the most important information
        3. Use headers to organize longer messages
        4. End with specific next steps
        5. Include relevant contact information
      </Steps>

      :::tip[Subject Line Formula]
      [Action Required/FYI] Brief Description - Deadline
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="All-Staff Meetings">
    <Card title="Meeting Guidelines" icon="people">
      <Steps>
        1. Send agenda in advance
        2. Begin with clear meeting objectives
        3. Use inclusive "we" language
        4. Allow time for Q&A
        5. End with action items summary
      </Steps>

      :::tip[Engagement Strategy]
      Include interactive elements like polls or breakout rooms for larger meetings
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Banyan Posts">
    <Card title="Social Strategy" icon="social">
      <Steps>
        1. Create engaging headlines
        2. Share authentic behind-the-scenes content
        3. Include clear calls to action
        4. Monitor and respond to comments
        5. Use inclusive language
      </Steps>

      :::tip[Content Mix]
      Balance formal updates with community-building content
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>

  <TabItem label="Newsletters">
    <Card title="Newsletter Structure" icon="document">
      <Steps>
        1. Maintain consistent sections
        2. Balance news with practical info
        3. Highlight diverse voices
        4. Include clear next steps
        5. Provide additional resources
      </Steps>

      :::tip[Template Tip]
      Create a reusable template with clear visual hierarchy
      :::
    </Card>
  </TabItem>
</Tabs>

## Quality Check & Resources

<CardGrid>
  <Card title="Pre-Send Checklist" icon="check">
    ### Before You Send
    <Steps>
      1. **Reliability Check**: Does this feel dependable and trustworthy?
      2. **Inclusivity Check**: Would all team members feel included?
      3. **Practicality Check**: Are next steps clear?
      4. **Honesty Check**: Are we being transparent?
      5. **Community Check**: Does this strengthen our shared goals?
    </Steps>
    
    :::caution[Important]
    If you answer "no" to any of these, revise before sending.
    :::
  </Card>
